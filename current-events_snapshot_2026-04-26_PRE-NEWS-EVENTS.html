My Study — Running Watchlist

🌍 Current Events Tracker

A running log of world events worth watching with a prophetic lens — filtered by category, not by panic.

Purpose

The goal here is discernment, not alarmism. World events in themselves prove nothing — but patterns of events, seen across years and measured against Scripture, can sharpen the watchman's eye. This page is where I track the stories that seem to matter, note what Scripture they connect to (if anything), and resist the temptation to force every headline into a prophetic frame.

Some events will turn out to be nothing. Some will be ordinary news that only felt significant in the moment. A few may end up genuinely significant. Only time and Scripture tell.

How to use this page
  1. Add events under the appropriate category below. Don't force-fit — if it doesn't cleanly belong to one, put it in General Watchlist.
  2. Include a date (when the event happened, not just when you heard about it).
  3. Write what happened in plain language — no speculation yet.
  4. Then, separately, note any Scripture it connects to — direct, thematic, or "maybe nothing."
  5. Revisit old entries periodically. Events that looked urgent often fade; events that looked minor sometimes compound. The log itself teaches discernment.

AI John Lennox releases God, AI and the End of History
2025 (UK release) · noted Apr 26, 2026
Oxford mathematician and Christian apologist John Lennox published a 600-page commentary on Revelation read through the lens of artificial intelligence. Unlike most AI-and-prophecy books, Lennox writes as a working mathematician with serious technical literacy, not as a prophecy-circuit speaker. His core argument is that a supernatural power opposed to God may be using advanced AI to drive a transhumanist vision of "upgraded humans," and that future AI developments could enable deception on a scale Scripture associates with the Antichrist. The book treats AI not primarily as a weapon but as a religion — a counterfeit path to immortality and godhood (Genesis 3:5 at industrial scale). His third AI book, after 2084 (2020) and the 2084 updated edition (2024), but the first to engage Revelation directly.
Scripture lens: Revelation 13 · 2 Thessalonians 2 · Genesis 3:5
Source: johnlennox.org — book page · SPCK Publishing
Relates to: Module 7 — The Antichrist · Resources page (candidate addition)
AI Chuck Pierce 2026 prophetic word — AI as a "spiritual crossroads"
Dec 22, 2025
In a Charisma Magazine feature, Chuck Pierce framed AI as one of the defining spiritual issues of 2026. He warned against passive surrender to "the robotic influence of artificial intelligence," presenting two paths: turning life over to AI by default, or rising into discernment and creativity. He paired this with a "Hezekiah, extend time" word — a five-year spiritual battle beginning around 2026, with what he described as a limited extension of mercy rather than immediate judgment. Notably, Pierce identified economics (not politics alone) as the central battleground — which lines up with the Revelation 13 mark-of-the-beast economic-control thread already on the watchlist. Caveat: this is a prophetic-word category source, not a systematic exposition. Useful as a watchman's prompt; weigh against Scripture, not the other way around.
Scripture lens: Revelation 13 · 2 Kings 20 (Hezekiah / extended time) · 1 John 4:1 (test the spirits)
Source: Charisma Magazine — Why 2026 Is Emerging as a Prophetic Turning Point
Relates to: Module 11 — False Prophets (testing framework) · Economy section above
AI Peer-reviewed paper — AI ethics & biblical prophecy framework (Sangwa & Mutabazi)
Jun 2025
An academic paper published on SSRN argues that AI's capacity for pervasive surveillance, information control, and reality-simulation can fulfill key elements of prophetic scenarios. The authors work from Revelation 13, 2 Thessalonians 2, and — interestingly — Genesis 11 (Babel) as a framework, fitting the pattern of humanity building toward heaven on its own terms. Specific concerns: algorithmic censorship, deepfakes, biometric surveillance, and transhumanism. Notable as a sign that the AI-eschatology conversation is no longer confined to prophecy circles — it's moving into peer-reviewed academic literature.
Scripture lens: Genesis 11 (Babel) · Revelation 13 · 2 Thessalonians 2 · Matthew 24:24 (deception)
Source: SSRN — Sangwa & Mutabazi (2025)
