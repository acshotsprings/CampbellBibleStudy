Watching the World Through Scripture

"You know how to interpret the appearance of the sky, but you cannot interpret the signs of the times." — Matthew 16:3

This section is not exegesis — it is observation. Current events do not interpret prophecy, but prophecy can help us understand the times we are living in. Each entry uses a real-world development as a launching pad for Spirit-led discussion, grounded in Scripture.

📌 How to use this section
Read the event. Consider the prophetic lens. Pray through the discussion questions. Write your observations in the notes box. Date every entry — your thinking will develop over time.
Remember: Hold these connections loosely. We are watching, not predicting. Only God knows the day and hour (Matt 24:36).
Entry #1

U.S. Energy Dominance & End-Times Geopolitics

Added: April 12, 2026

📰 What's Happening
The U.S. is aggressively expanding offshore oil production in the Gulf of America, with major new discoveries (Occidental Petroleum's "Bandit" well off the Louisiana coast — one of the deepest ever drilled) and 30 mandated federal lease sales under new legislation. America is positioning itself as a dominant global energy superpower.

🔍 Prophetic Lenses

Ezekiel 38–39 — The Gog-Magog Invasion
A key scholarly question is why the Gog-Magog coalition invades Israel. Ezekiel 38:12–13 suggests they come for "plunder and spoil" — Israel's wealth and resources. If U.S. energy dominance shifts global power and reduces Russian and Iranian leverage over the world economy, it could accelerate the desperation that drives those nations toward the coalition described in Ezekiel 38. Worth watching.
Key passages: Ezek 38:1–6 (coalition), 38:10–13 (motive), 39:1–6 (outcome)
America's Role — Or Absence — in Prophecy
One of the great prophetic questions is why the United States appears absent from end-times Scripture. Some scholars suggest America's power will be diminished, distracted, or collapsed before the Tribulation begins. Others see the U.S. as part of the "young lions" in Ezekiel 38:13 — protesting the Gog-Magog invasion but not intervening militarily. Either way, watching American strength and global posture is worth tracking through a prophetic lens.
Key passages: Ezek 38:13 (young lions), Rev 17:18 (great city), Dan 7:4 (eagle's wings)
Revelation 17–18 — Mystery Babylon
Some prophecy scholars interpret "Mystery Babylon" as a dominant commercial and economic power — not ancient Babylon literally, but a system or nation characterized by global trade dominance, wealth, and influence. Aggressive resource extraction and energy dominance are characteristics worth noting — not as proof of any identification, but as a discussion thread worth holding loosely.
Key passages: Rev 17:1–6, 18:1–8, 18:11–19
🙏 Discussion Questions
As the U.S. expands energy dominance, how might this shift the global power dynamics described in Ezekiel 38? Could it make the Gog-Magog invasion more or less likely in the near term?
What role does Scripture suggest America plays — or doesn't play — in the last days? Does current U.S. strength make that harder or easier to imagine?
How do we stay watchful without over-interpreting? What's the difference between "reading the signs of the times" and sensationalism or fear?
What does 1 Thessalonians 5:1–6 say about how we should live in light of the uncertainty of timing?
