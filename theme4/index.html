Theme 4 — Overview

Covenant

God's binding promises through Noah, Abraham, Moses, David, and Jesus

📖 What this theme is about
The thread that runs through the entire Bible.

A covenant is not a contract. A contract is a deal between equals; either side can walk away if the other side fails. A covenant is a binding promise rooted in the greater party's character, sealed by oath, attached to a sign, and impossible to renegotiate. It does not depend on the lesser party's performance. It depends on the One who swore it.

Theme 3 ended with the Noahic covenant — God's first explicit binding promise to creation. Theme 4 picks up that thread and follows it forward through Scripture: Abraham (the call out and the seed promised), Moses (the law given and the nation formed), David (the kingdom and the throne that endures), and Jesus (the New Covenant in His blood, the fulfillment of all the rest). Each builds on the pattern Noah saw. Each is made by God's free initiative. Each one points forward to Christ.

If you want to understand how the Bible holds together as one story rather than 66 separate books, the covenant pattern is the spine. Theme 4 walks that spine.
