Theme 2: Biblical Calendar & Prophecy

God designed time itself — and the calendar may be the key that unlocks prophecy

The Big Idea
Most of us read the Bible through a calendar we inherited from Greece, Rome, and medieval popes. But God gave Israel a different calendar — a perfect 364-day solar calendar used from Adam through David. When you read prophecy through that original calendar, the numbers in Daniel, Revelation, and the Feasts of Israel snap into place with stunning precision. This theme is about recovering that original framework.
Genesis 1:14 (NLT)
Then God said, "Let lights appear in the sky to separate the day from the night. Let them be signs to mark the seasons, days, and years."

God built a timekeeping system into creation itself — sun, moon, and stars as precision instruments marking His appointed times. The question this theme explores: What calendar did God have in mind? And why does the answer matter more than most people realize?

Why This Theme Exists

This theme grew directly out of a sermon series on the Dead Sea Scrolls and the writings of Ken Johnson (April 2026). As we studied the calendar debate — Essenes vs. Pharisees, solar vs. lunar, 364-day vs. 354-day — a key insight emerged:

🔑 The Core Insight
If the feasts were ordained by God on a 364-day solar calendar, and we are calculating prophetic fulfillment against a 354-day lunar calendar, we may be systematically misreading God's prophetic timeline.
The 1,260 days of Revelation 11:3 and 12:6 don't align cleanly to 3½ years on a lunar calendar — but they do on a 360-day prophetic year derived from the original solar framework. The feasts in Leviticus 23 are "appointed times" (mo'edim) — precision appointments on God's calendar, not cultural suggestions.
This is not about discarding Bible study — it's about reading Scripture in its original framework and letting it become more accurate, not less.

The Three Calendars in Play

Calendar Comparison at a Glance
The Essene Solar Calendar (God's original): 364 days · 52 exact weeks · Every feast on the same weekday every year · Used from Adam through the Second Temple period · Preserved in the Dead Sea Scrolls, Book of Jubilees, and Book of Enoch
The Pharisee Lunar Calendar: 354 days · Months follow the moon · New Year in the fall (Tishrei) · Adopted under Greek pressure during the Maccabean period · Codified by Rabbi Hillel II in the 4th century AD · The calendar modern Judaism uses today
The Gregorian Calendar (our daily calendar): 365.25 days · Pure solar, but not tied to God's feasts · Shaped by Julius Caesar and Pope Gregory XIII · Shifts holy days off their original weekday anchors

📌 Why 1,260 Days Matters
1,260 days appears twice in Revelation (11:3 and 12:6) and refers to the second half of the Tribulation (also called "42 months" and "a time, times, and half a time"). On a 360-day prophetic year — derived from the original solar calendar — this equals exactly 3½ years (3.5 × 360 = 1,260). The math works with God's calendar. It gets complicated with ours.

How This Theme Connects to the End Times
Daniel's 70 Weeks (Module 1): The 70 "weeks" (490 years) only calculate precisely when using the 360-day prophetic year derived from the original solar calendar.
The Tribulation Timeline (Modules 6–8): The 1,260 days, 42 months, and "time, times, and half a time" all refer to the same 3½-year period — and only align mathematically using the 360-day year.

A Personal Reflection

When this study first opened up in April 2026, the reaction was something like: "The deeper I get on this, the more I realize we have twisted the true meaning of what God had in mind when He invented time. It makes me want to go back — so that I understand the Word better."

That's exactly the right response. We inherited a calendar shaped by Greek politics, Roman power, and papal reform. The Essenes went to the desert and preserved the original framework at enormous cost — because they understood what was at stake. This study is an attempt to read Scripture in the framework it was written in.
