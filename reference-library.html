Apocryphal & Second Temple Texts
Non-canonical writings referenced in this study — not Scripture, but historically significant for understanding prophecy and calendar context
Book of Enoch (1 Enoch)
Second Temple
An ancient Jewish apocalyptic text attributed to Enoch (Gen 5:24). Contains the Astronomical Book (chs. 72–82) describing a 364-day solar calendar, the Watchers narrative, and visions of judgment. Quoted directly in Jude 1:14–15. Foundational to Dead Sea Scrolls calendar research.
Calendar364-Day SolarDead Sea ScrollsProphecy
📖 Read Online Ch. 72 — Solar Calendar
Book of Jubilees
Second Temple
Also called "Lesser Genesis." Retells Genesis and Exodus emphasizing the 364-day solar calendar as God's eternal design. Explicitly commands observance of the solar calendar (Jubilees 6:36–38). Found among the Dead Sea Scrolls in more copies than any book except Psalms, Deuteronomy, and Isaiah.

Damascus Document
Dead Sea Scrolls
Also called the "Zadokite Fragments." Written by the Qumran/Essene community, it describes the corruption of the Jerusalem priesthood under Antiochus Epiphanes, and the covenant of the Sons of Zadok who withdrew to preserve the true calendar and true worship.

Josephus — Antiquities of the Jews
Historical
First-century Jewish historian invaluable for Second Temple period context. Confirmed that Romans noted Jews keeping "Saturnalia" every Saturday — corroborating the unbroken seven-day Sabbath cycle. Also records the corruption of the high priesthood under Antiochus Epiphanes.
