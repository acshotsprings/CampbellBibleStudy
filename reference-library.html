📜 Apocryphal & Dead Sea Scrolls Texts 4 resources
Book of Enoch (1 Enoch)
Apocryphal · Second Temple Period
The primary ancient source for the 364-day solar calendar. Enoch 72 describes the sun's path and the 364-day year in precise detail. The Essenes treated it as authoritative. Referenced throughout Theme 2 calendar modules and Theme 1 prophecy calculations.

Book of Jubilees
Apocryphal · Second Temple Period
Retells Genesis through Exodus from the perspective of the 364-day solar calendar. Jubilees 6:36–38 is the key passage warning Israel never to abandon the solar calendar or the feasts will fall on wrong days. Central to Theme 2, Module 3.

Dead Sea Scrolls — Key Texts
Archaeological · ~150 BC – 68 AD
The Qumran community preserved the 364-day solar calendar in multiple scrolls. Key calendar texts: 4Q318, 4Q394, the Damascus Document, and the Community Rule. The Essenes called themselves the Sons of Light and preserved the Zadokite priestly tradition.

Josephus — Jewish Antiquities & Wars
Historical · 1st Century AD
Flavius Josephus was a first-century Jewish historian who recorded the destruction of the Temple, the Essene community, Pharisee practices, and Roman-Jewish history. Referenced for calendar history, the high priesthood, and Yoma 9a (high priest mortality).

Eusebius — Ecclesiastical History
Historical · ~313 AD
The earliest comprehensive history of the Christian church, covering the apostolic era through Constantine. Useful for understanding how early church calendars developed and diverged from Jewish tradition.
