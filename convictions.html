April 9, 2026 — Gog, Magog & the Russia-Iran Coalition (Module 9 Deep Dive)
Sources: Module 9 (Ezekiel 38–39), Joel Rosenberg — The Ezekiel Option Ch. 7, current news search (April 9, 2026)
What Was Studied:
Module 9 of the End Times study covers Ezekiel 38–39 — the Gog-Magog War. This session began with a personal question about who Gog is and where Magog is, prompted by a reflection written on the Convictions page.
Key Finding — Gog Is a Title, Not a Name:
Joel Rosenberg argues that "Gog" is most likely a title — like Tsar or Pharaoh — not a personal name. We are not looking for someone literally named Gog, but a ruler who arises from the land of Magog, described by Ezekiel as "the remote parts of the north" (Ezekiel 38:15).
Key Finding — Magog = Russia (Five-Step Evidence Case):
1. Geographic: Only five nations lie due north of Israel — Lebanon, Syria, Turkey, Ukraine, Russia. Russia is the farthest north by significant distance.
2. Hostile Witness: Voltaire — an enemy of Christianity — independently concluded Magog referred to Russia about 150 years before Russia became a world power, tracing it through the Genesis 10 genealogy.
3. Table of Nations: Genesis 10:1–2 lists Magog as a son of Japheth. Ancient and medieval tradition consistently linked Japheth's descendants to northern Eurasian regions — modern Russia and Central Asia.
4. Pre-Modern Scholarship: Scholars in 1846 — before the Soviet era — reached the Russia identification independently, removing the objection that it is motivated by contemporary geopolitics.
5. Contemporary Profile: Rosenberg wrote from Moscow in 2004 documenting Putin's early consolidation of power. His core argument: a strong authoritarian northern ruler with "hooks in his jaws" pulled toward catastrophic miscalculation against Israel matches Russia's trajectory.
Personal Question Raised — and a Good One:
During this session, the question was raised: if Ezekiel is writing prophecy — not just describing what he knew — does it matter that Russia wasn't the farthest north nation known to him? The answer: Ezekiel saw the vision and described it in terms available to him. The revelation was designed for the people living when these nations actually exist as world powers. The point is not what Ezekiel knew — it is what God showed him. Prophecy consistently describes things the prophet could not have fully understood (gas and oil under Israel being another example).
The Coalition — Ancient Names, Modern Nations:
Rosh / Magog = Russia / Central Asia  ·  Persia = Iran  ·  Cush = Sudan / Ethiopia  ·  Put = Libya  ·  Gomer / Beth-togarmah = Turkey / Central Asia
Current Events Search — April 9, 2026:
Russia is actively providing Iran with satellite intelligence on Israeli and American military targets — detailed imagery of 55 critical Israeli energy infrastructure sites. Iran's Foreign Minister stated publicly that military cooperation between Russia and Iran is "not something new" and is continuing. Russia has struck deals to supply Iran with advanced air defense systems in 2027–2029. Russian and Iranian hackers are collaborating in the cyber domain. This is happening during an active U.S.-Israel conflict with Iran that began February 28, 2026.
Personal Reaction:
This does not hit differently so much as it rattles the cage. The realization that the coalition Ezekiel described 2,500 years ago is visibly forming right now creates a sense of urgency. The unknown is not whether this will happen — only how many years remain before the coalition is strong enough to act.
God's Purpose in the Gog-Magog War:
Ezekiel 38:23 — "In this way, I will show my greatness and holiness, and I will make myself known to all the nations of the world. Then they will know that I am the LORD." God defeats this massive coalition without Israel firing a single shot. The purpose is not Israel's military victory — it is God's self-revelation to the nations. Even in catastrophe, He has a redemptive purpose.
