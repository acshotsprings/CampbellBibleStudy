Topic: A Conversation with Natalie — The Firmament, Israel & Teaching Your Kids What You're Learning
Category: Family / Creation & Cosmology / Israel in Prophecy / End Times Readiness
Had a really meaningful conversation today with Natalie — my daughter, Toopie's twin, living in Kansas City. She had been reading about the firmament and had already talked to Pastor Matt about it, which made the conversation even richer. She brought it to me wanting to dig deeper, and the three of us — her, me, and Claude — worked through it together. That made me smile, especially given what came next.
Natalie does not like AI. She is convinced it is taking jobs from people and she's not shy about saying so. The irony of using it to answer her Bible question was not lost on either of us. But I'll say this — when the tool helps you understand Scripture better and brings a father and daughter closer over the Word of God, it's hard to argue with the result. We had a good laugh about it.
On the Firmament (Genesis 1:6–8): The Hebrew word is rakia — literally "something beaten out" or "spread out," like hammering metal into a sheet. On Day 2 of creation, God separated the waters — some below (our oceans) and some above (held back by this firmament-dome). The sky we see is that expanse. When the firmament "opened" in Genesis 7 during the flood, the waters above came down — which is why Scripture describes the flood as coming from both above and below simultaneously. It was cosmic-scale catastrophe, not just heavy rain. There is also a direct connection to End Times prophecy — Revelation 6:14 describes the sky "splitting like a scroll," which many scholars connect back to this same cosmic boundary language from Genesis. What God established at creation, He will one day undo and remake entirely. This is exactly where Theme 3 of this study guide is heading — God's pattern of establishing order, warning, judgment, and rescue from Genesis to Revelation.
On Israel and Palestine: Natalie came into the conversation with a question that a lot of sincere believers struggle with: if Israel is God's chosen people, why are they killing others? It's the right question to ask and it deserves an honest answer. I walked her through the timeline — October 7, 2023, Hamas launching the largest massacre of Jewish people since the Holocaust, over 1,200 killed in a single day, hostages taken including children and the elderly. Israel's military response was not aggression — it was a response to an act of war launched by a terrorist organization that uses civilians as human shields and has stated openly that its goal is the complete destruction of the Jewish state. That context changes everything.
But the deeper point I wanted her to grasp is the prophetic one. These things are not random. They are not simply geopolitical conflict. Scripture is clear that in the last days all nations will be gathered against Jerusalem (Zechariah 14:2). The pressure on Israel will increase, not decrease. The world's hostility toward God's covenant people is itself a sign. And Israel's national repentance — their eventual cry for the Messiah they rejected — is what triggers the Second Coming (Zechariah 12:10; Matthew 23:39). You cannot understand what is happening in the Middle East right now without understanding the prophetic framework. And you cannot understand the prophetic framework without loving and praying for Israel.
By the end of the conversation Natalie had a different perspective. She still cares deeply about the suffering of civilians — and she should, and so do I — but she now understands that caring for the innocent and standing with Israel are not mutually exclusive. And she understands that what we are watching unfold is not a surprise to God. It was written 2,500 years ago.
This is exactly why I am building this study guide. Not just for me — but so I have something to hand to my kids when these questions come up. So that conversations like this one have roots to grow from.

Topic: Grace, Truth & Witness in the Last Days
Category: Evangelism / Christian Living / Signs of the Times
Came across the Jaden Ivey situation today — the NBA player waived by the Chicago Bulls after publicly calling out the league's Pride Month promotion as "unrighteous." He's clearly sincere in his faith and the biblical convictions he holds are real. Scripture is clear on the subject: Leviticus 18:22, Romans 1:26–27, 1 Corinthians 6:9. The question this raises isn't whether the conviction is biblical — it is — but whether the expression of that conviction was the most effective witness in that moment.
Jesus was full of both grace AND truth (John 1:14). Neither one at the expense of the other. He called sin what it was, but he also ate with sinners, healed them, and spoke to them with love before and after correction. The Pharisees had truth without grace. The culture today often pushes grace without truth. Both are distortions.
What strikes me most is the cost. This young man lost his career — or at least accelerated its end — over a public statement of faith. That's not nothing. And yet this is exactly what Jesus said would happen: "You will be hated by all for my name's sake." (Matt 10:22)
Key scriptures: John 1:14, Matthew 10:22, Romans 1:16, Colossians 4:5–6.

Topic: Grace, Holiness & the Danger of Carnal Christianity
Category: Christian Living / Church Health / End Times Readiness
Came across a powerful Facebook post from Pastor Larry Burton today that addresses something increasingly relevant in the end times context — the misuse of grace as a license to continue in sin. His central argument is compelling: Grace isn't a lowered bar — it's a higher one, backed by better resources (the Holy Spirit, the new covenant, Christ's indwelling). Using grace as a license to sin inverts its entire purpose.
This connects directly to the end-times framework. Jesus himself warns in Matthew 7:21–23 that He will say to many who did ministry in His name: "I never knew you; depart from me, you workers of iniquity." A church full of people who claim grace but live in sin is completely unprepared for the deception to come.
Pastor Burton's definition of repentance is worth noting: not just feeling sorry, but a change of attitude, behavior, direction, affections, and relationships. Christ is coming for a pure Bride. Five of the seven churches in Revelation are told to repent.
Key scriptures: Heb 12:14, Ezek 18:24, Rom 6:1–2, Rev 3:5.

Topic: Spiritual Deception & the Demonic Realm
Category: Signs of the Times / Spiritual Warfare
Came across something today that connected directly to what we've been studying. VP JD Vance made a public statement arguing that unexplained aerial phenomena may not be extraterrestrial at all, but something from the spiritual realm. He specifically referenced the Christian understanding that evil is real and active — that one of the devil's greatest tricks is convincing the world he doesn't exist.
This strikes me as deeply connected to what we've been studying about deception in the end times. Scripture is clear that the enemy doesn't announce himself — he masquerades. 2 Corinthians 11:14 says that Satan disguises himself as an angel of light. If demonic activity can be reframed as a cosmic, non-threatening mystery — something humanity is meant to "discover" — that is exactly the kind of deception Jesus warned about in Matthew 24.
Key scriptures: 2 Corinthians 11:14, Matthew 24:24, Revelation 13:13–14, Ephesians 6:12.
